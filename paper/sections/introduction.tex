\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The global population aged 60 years and older is projected to double by 2050, exceeding 2 billion individuals. Simultaneously, the World Health Organization anticipates a shortage of 10 million skilled health professionals by 2030. Physical rehabilitation is particularly affected; in numerous low- and middle-income countries, the physiotherapist-to-population ratio is less than 1 per 100,000. Consequently, millions of elderly patients lack access to supervised exercise therapy for conditions including stroke recovery, osteoarthritis, and fall prevention.

Computer vision-based rehabilitation systems have shown potential to improve physiotherapy services~\cite{chen2020pose}. However, existing systems face several challenges in serving elderly users: (1) dependence on expensive depth sensors such as Kinect or LiDAR, which are typically unavailable in home settings; (2) exclusive reliance on body-only 2D pose estimation, neglecting hand gestures and facial expressions that are critical for assessing exercise form; (3) absence of natural-language voice interfaces suitable for individuals with limited digital literacy; (4) inadequate adaptation to elderly-specific requirements, such as reduced mobility, heightened pain sensitivity, and slower exercise pace; and (5) generic feedback mechanisms that fail to consider individual clinical conditions.

We introduce \system{}, a multimodal AI rehabilitation system designed to address these barriers. We explicitly define the scope: this paper presents a \emph{systems feasibility} study that demonstrates that the integration is computationally practical and architecturally robust, rather than providing clinical validation. Our contributions are as follows:

\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=2pt, topsep=3pt, parsep=0pt]
    \item \textbf{Kinematics and scoring algorithm pipeline}: Real-time whole-body 3D pose estimation via RTMW3D-L~\cite{jiang2024rtmw} from MMPose~\cite{contributors2020openmmlab}, with quaternion-based joint angle computation, SPARC smoothness~\cite{Scholp2021}, constrained DTW alignment, and temporal compensation detection integrated into a clinically-inspired six-dimension scoring framework.

    \item \textbf{Multi-indicator facial state detection}: A face analysis pipeline leveraging RTMW3D's 68 face landmarks and OpenFace~3.0 Action Unit detection, integrating PSPI-based pain estimation~\cite{prkachin2008structure}, PERCLOS-based fatigue monitoring, blink and yawn detection, and engagement analysis~\cite{6786307}, with automatic fallback to geometric analysis.

    \item \textbf{Scoring framework with public benchmark validation}: Evaluation of the proposed scoring framework on two public rehabilitation benchmarks, achieving significant clinical correlation on KIMORE (Spearman $\rho = 0.347$, $p < 10^{-11}$) and near-perfect exercise discriminability on UCO (AUC = 0.974), with position DTW outperforming angle DTW by an order of magnitude.
\end{enumerate}

The remainder of this paper is organized as follows: Section~\ref{sec:related} reviews related work. Section~\ref{sec:methodology} details our system architecture and algorithms. Section~\ref{sec:experiments} reports scoring-framework validation on public benchmarks. Section~\ref{sec:discussion} discusses design rationale and limitations. Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes with future directions.
