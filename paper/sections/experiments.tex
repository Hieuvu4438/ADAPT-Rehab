\section{Experimental Setup and Evaluation}
\label{sec:experiments}

We position \system{} as a \emph{systems feasibility} study: the goal of this evaluation is to establish that real-time, whole-body, multimodal rehabilitation analysis is achievable on a single consumer GPU, and to characterize honestly which components are mature and which require further validation. We therefore report (1)~temporal stability of the pose pipeline and (2)~scoring validation on two public rehabilitation benchmarks. We explicitly do \emph{not} claim clinical-grade joint-angle accuracy: as detailed below, the absence of metric-scale ground truth on our exercise data means our pose metrics measure self-consistency, not error against motion-capture references.

\subsection{Datasets}
\begin{itemize}[itemsep=2pt, topsep=3pt, parsep=0pt]
    \item \textbf{KIMORE}~\cite{Capecci2019}: 378 recordings across 5 rehabilitation exercises from 78 subjects (44 healthy, 34 with motor dysfunctions), captured with Kinect~v2 (25-joint skeleton). Each recording carries a clinical Total Score (cTS) from physical therapists. Used for clinical correlation and component ablation.
    \item \textbf{UCO Physical Rehabilitation}~\cite{AguilarOrtega2023}: 393 recordings of 16 exercises from 27 subjects, captured with 5 synchronized RGB cameras and an OptiTrack motion-capture ground-truth system ($\pm$0.5\,mm accuracy). 1{,}918 per-repetition clinician scores (2--5 scale). Used for exercise discrimination and cross-view analysis.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Pose Estimation Temporal Stability}
We report \emph{temporal self-consistency}: the mean per-joint deviation of each frame's 3D keypoints from the per-video temporal mean, quantifying frame-to-frame jitter and tracking stability. On UI-PRMD, this self-consistency measure over 1{,}423 Kinect-skeleton samples yields $0.18 \pm 0.22$\,mm deviation, confirming the mapping framework runs end-to-end across skeleton formats with sub-millimetre jitter.

\subsection{Scoring Validation on KIMORE}
We evaluate the scoring framework on KIMORE using a fixed-reference protocol: the top-10 clinically-scored recordings per exercise serve as expert templates (mirroring clinical practice where a physiotherapist demonstration is the reference), and Procrustes-aligned position DTW computes the similarity of each recording to the nearest expert template.

Table~\ref{tab:kimore_corr} reports the Spearman correlation between our score and the clinical Total Score. All five exercises show significant positive correlation ($p < 0.05$), with an overall $\rho = 0.347$ ($p < 10^{-11}$).

\textbf{DTW variant comparison.} Procrustes-aligned position DTW ($\rho = 0.400$) substantially outperforms angle-based DTW ($\rho = 0.042$), a $9.5\times$ improvement. This confirms that body-level spatial alignment captures rehabilitation movement quality better than per-joint angle trajectories alone.

\subsection{Exercise Discrimination on UCO}
We evaluate exercise-level discriminability on UCO using 6{,}000 balanced same-exercise vs.\ different-exercise pairs across 16 exercise classes. Table~\ref{tab:uco_discrim} reports a same-vs-different AUC of $0.974$ with a 48-point mean separation, confirming that the scoring framework reliably distinguishes correct exercise performance from incorrect cross-exercise comparisons.

\begin{table}[htbp]
    \caption{UCO Exercise Discrimination (16 classes, 6000 pairs)}

    \centering

        \midrule
        AUC-ROC & 0.974 \\
        Same-exercise mean score & $52.9 \pm 19.8$ \\
        Different-exercise mean score & $4.7 \pm 9.1$ \\
        Separation & 48.2 points \\
        \bottomrule

\end{table}

\textbf{Cross-view analysis.} Silhouette-based motion signals exhibit an 87-point similarity drop across the five camera views, reinforcing the motivation for direct 3D pose estimation in rehabilitation settings where camera placement varies.
