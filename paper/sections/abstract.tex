\begin{abstract}
Population aging is accelerating globally: the proportion of adults aged 60 years and older is projected to double by 2050, while physiotherapist-to-population ratios remain critically low in low- and middle-income countries. This work presents \system{}, a multimodal AI rehabilitation support system designed for elderly users, and reports a feasibility study of the system. The system comprises three main components. First, a kinematics and scoring algorithm pipeline integrates real-time whole-body 3D pose estimation via RTMW3D-L~\cite{jiang2024rtmw} from MMPose~\cite{contributors2020openmmlab} with quaternion-based kinematics, SPARC smoothness~\cite{Scholp2021}, constrained DTW alignment, and temporal compensation detection. Second, multi-indicator facial state detection uses RTMW3D face landmarks together with OpenFace 3.0 Action Unit analysis, combining PSPI-inspired pain estimation~\cite{prkachin2008structure}, PERCLOS-based fatigue monitoring, and engagement detection~\cite{6786307}. Third, the scoring framework is evaluated on two public rehabilitation benchmarks, yielding a moderate but statistically significant correlation with clinical scores on KIMORE (Spearman $\rho = 0.347$, $p < 10^{-11}$) and strong exercise discriminability on UCO (AUC = 0.974). The results are intended as a systems-feasibility demonstration rather than a clinical validation; clinical evaluation and per-component calibration are identified as necessary next steps.
\end{abstract}
