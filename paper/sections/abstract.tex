\begin{abstract}
Population aging is accelerating globally: the proportion of adults aged 60 years and older is projected to double by 2050, while physiotherapist-to-population ratios remain critically low in low- and middle-income countries. This work introduces \system{}, a multimodal AI rehabilitation system designed for elderly users, and reports a feasibility study of the system. The system offers three primary contributions: (1) a kinematics and scoring algorithm pipeline that integrates real-time whole-body 3D pose estimation via RTMW3D-L~\cite{jiang2024rtmw} from MMPose~\cite{contributors2020openmmlab} with quaternion-based kinematics, SPARC smoothness~\cite{Scholp2021}, constrained DTW alignment, and temporal compensation detection; (2) multi-indicator facial state detection using RTMW3D face landmarks and OpenFace 3.0 Action Unit analysis, which combines PSPI-inspired pain estimation~\cite{prkachin2008structure}, PERCLOS-based fatigue monitoring, and engagement detection~\cite{6786307}; and (3) a scoring framework validated on two public rehabilitation benchmarks, demonstrating significant clinical correlation on KIMORE (Spearman $\rho = 0.347$, $p < 10^{-11}$) and near-perfect exercise discriminability on UCO (AUC = 0.974). Clinical validation and scoring calibration are identified as essential next steps.
\end{abstract}
